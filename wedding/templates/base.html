The location

Schloss Atzelsberg

Dress code: summer chic

Our Polterabend starts with apéritif and hors d'oeuvres at 6 PM. Enjoy some bubbly from the Champagne House J. de Telmont. Did you know that Michael studied in the Champagne region? His first entrepreneurial venture was importing J. de Telmont from France to Germany.

 

Just before sunset at 8 PM, we all gather in front of the Castle's barn, ready to smash old plates, bowls, and cups. The smashing of china is the highlight of a traditional Polterabend. Watch Karen and Michael demonstrate their amazing teamwork as they sweep up all the shards!

 

After Karen and Michael clean up the smashed porcelain, we all head to the Castle's banquet hall at 9 PM to enjoy a Franconian dinner. After dinner, DJ Marcus (Franconia's most sought-after DJ!) spins hip hop for Michael and EDM for Karen.

The location

Entlas Keller

Be at Entlas Keller, an Erlangen favorite, at 1 PM on Saturday. Frühschoppen, plus a special tour of Entlas Keller’s beer cellar, await.

 

Frühschoppen (meaning: an alcoholic drink before midday in company) is the German and Austrian tradition to meet up at a pub in the late morning, traditionally held after church service on Sundays.

Frühschoppen is often a kind of brunch, involving the consumption of alcoholic beverages. In keeping with tradition, join us as we sit under old chestnut trees and enjoy a Maß of beer and Franconian culinary classics.

 

At 2 PM, tour the Entlas Keller cellar with us. Visit the cave and get to see part of the 21 km tunnel dug into the hill to keep the beer cold.
